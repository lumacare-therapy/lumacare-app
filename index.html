The 5-Minute Priority Matrix Method

When stress makes everything feel urgent, this method clears the noise.

1

Brain Dump (1 minute)

Set a timer. Open a blank note and write down every single task, email, and nagging thought. Don't judge, don't organize. The messier, the better.

2

Sort (3 minutes)

Draw a 2x2 grid. For each item, ask two questions:

  • "Is this truly urgent?" (time-sensitive)
  • "Is this truly important?" (aligns with your goals)

Force every item into one box:

Urgent & Important

Do it NOW

Important / Not Urgent

Schedule it

Urgent / Not Important

Delegate or batch

Neither

Delete it

3

First Move (1 minute)

Ignore 75% of the grid. Look only at the Urgent & Important box. Pick the one task that would make everything else feel easier. Do a 5-minute sprint on just that.

This app's "Priority Matrix" tool (in the Techniques tab) automates this sorting for you.

Tip: The power isn't in complexity. It's in forcing a clear choice for every single task. This transforms emotional overwhelm into logical decisions.
